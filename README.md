# 📊 Employee Attrition Analysis using Power BI

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using Microsoft Power BI. The objective is to identify key factors contributing to employee turnover and provide HR leaders with actionable insights for improving workforce retention.

The dashboard was developed as part of a Business Analyst internship project and demonstrates end-to-end data analysis, dashboard development, and business storytelling.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Calculate overall employee attrition rate
- Identify departments and job roles with the highest attrition
- Analyze demographic trends (Age, Gender, Education, Marital Status)
- Study tenure and career-stage attrition
- Evaluate the impact of overtime, business travel, work-life balance, and environment satisfaction
- Provide HR recommendations based on data-driven insights

## 📊 Key Insights

- Overall Attrition Rate: **16.12%**
- Research & Development experienced the highest employee attrition.
- Employees aged **26–35** had the highest attrition.
- Single employees left more frequently than married employees.
- Employees working overtime showed higher attrition.
- Early-tenure employees (0–2 years) experienced the highest attrition.
- Longer promotion gaps were associated with increased attrition.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Strengthen onboarding and mentoring programs.
- Reduce excessive overtime to improve employee well-being.
- Improve career progression and promotion planning.
- Focus retention strategies on high-risk departments and job roles.
- Enhance work-life balance initiatives.
